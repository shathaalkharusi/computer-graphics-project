Line drawing algorithm:

In computer graphics, a method for approximating a straight line segment on pixel-based displays (like monitors or printers). It calculates which pixels to illuminate to create the visually closest representation of the line, considering the limitations of the discrete display grid. This allows for the creation of smooth-looking lines despite individual pixels.





Useful Definitions General Requirements
Rasterization:
Process of determining which pixels provide the best approximation to a desired line on the screen.
Scan Conversion:
Combination of rasterization and generating the picture in scan line order.
  1. Straight lines must appear as straight lines

  2. They must start and end accurately

  3. Lines should have constant brightness along their length

  4. Lines should be drawn rapidly
